Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Horizon House — our homes, our mission, and how we support recovery.

Horizon House is a residence where individuals commit to living in a drug- and alcohol-free environment. We provide a structured, supportive community for people maintaining sobriety, whether they are transitioning from treatment programs or voluntarily choosing this lifestyle. Our homes are not treatment facilities but rather safe, substance-free spaces that promote accountability and personal growth.

Horizon House is a residence where individuals live in a drug- and alcohol-free environment. We provide a supportive community for people committed to sobriety, whether they are transitioning from treatment programs or voluntarily choosing this lifestyle.


Horizon House is not a treatment facility and does not provide clinical services. Instead, we offer structure, accountability, and peer support to help residents maintain their recovery.


Key elements that ensure safety include:

  • Clean Water & Stable Utilities – Reliable electricity, heating, cooling, and clean water provide a comfortable and hygienic living space.
  • Drug- and Alcohol-Free Environment – Strict zero-tolerance policies ensure that all residents remain sober, creating a stable and supportive atmosphere.
  • House Rules & Accountability – Curfews, regular drug testing, and clear expectations help residents stay on track in their recovery journey.
  • Supportive Staff & Peer Community – Trained staff or house managers provide guidance, structure, and conflict resolution, while residents support one another through shared experiences.
  • Safe & Well-Maintained Property – Regular upkeep, quiet hours, and neighborhood-friendly policies ensure a peaceful and respectful living environment.

Residents of Horizon House come from diverse backgrounds. All of our residents voluntarily choose to live in our homes and maintain a commitment to abstinence from drugs and alcohol. Our homes provide a peaceful and supportive space for individuals pursuing a healthy, substance-free lifestyle. The average length of stay in our homes is about 12 months.

No special license or permit is required because Horizon House does not provide clinical treatment or operate as a healthcare facility. Under federal and California fair housing laws, our homes are considered single-family residences and must be treated the same as any other home in the neighborhood.

Horizon House operates under house rules that all residents must follow, such as maintaining sobriety, contributing to household responsibilities, and respecting neighbors. Our homes have resident leaders or house managers who oversee daily operations and ensure the home remains safe and orderly.
